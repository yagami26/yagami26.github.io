K-P opposition patterns in Chess

Posted on Mon 03 February 2025 in Chess

Opposition is a concept I always knew, but never really appreciated the beauty of it. Basically a king gains opposition, when it forces other king to move and not occupy an important square.

Take a look at this study for eg

Here is another study to appreciate the opposition concept.

As a general rule I think we should notice the following:

  • It's bad for enemy king to step in front of our promoting pawn
  • It's bad for our king be forced to lose opposition, because losing opposition forces us to move away.

Apart from that as far as chess update goes, I think I really enjoyed analyzing my game in classical chess. I believe classical chess is the way for me to improve and appreciate chess better. I have this tendency to make moves instinctively. Take this study of endgames for example, I spent some time today and drilled down the concept slightly better than I had yesterday.

It was also interesting to do a write up of my game analysis I played as a part of Dojo (my online chess club). I had some insights. Mostly I believe I need to calm the fuck down during games and do the hard work (calculation :P), then evaluate the position and calculate variations of other candidate moves. Visualization is an exercise I think will develop with my Polgar mate exercises.

I believe this study will definitely be easier for me to review later. Ciao!
