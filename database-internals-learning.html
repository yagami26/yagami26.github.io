Let's build a database

Posted on Sun 01 August 2021 in database

Background

I have always liked the idea of doing small pet projects and making something. Back when I was an undergrad, I always used to make small utilities and pet projects about almost anything I found interesting.

It was as simple as automation to download files using a python script and wget. Crawling anime sites, downloading mangas, filtering out canon episodes, making a small search engine etc. It would not be wrong to say I used to get an adrenaline rush when I used to build stuff.

Now, it has almost been 6 years since I graduated from university and I haven't built anything significant, maybe because I got lazy. For few days now, I seem to be getting back to my curious self.

Let's build a toy database

Why ?

I have multiple reasons to pick this as a new project let me explain why

  • Databases are used almost everywhere. Think about it mobile apps, websites, desktop applications. At front end layer ? I'm not sure but a quick google search does show a few results.
  • Databases come in many flavours and each of which seems very interesting Relational, NoSQL, Time Series, Wide Column Stores, Graph, GIS databases
  • Most developers know a high level understanding of databases and maybe they can conclude to a reasonable level which database to use. However how many know how are transactions are handled, how is the consistency maintained in distributed setup. I have been using databases daily, why not get a deeper understanding of how they work.
  • To explore more hardcore engineering related roles. Frankly, I have been building CRUD API's and backend systems for almost 4 years now and it doesn't seem that challenging to me personally. Not saying it is easy by any means or that I am some pro. But I don't find it that interesting now a days.

How ?

The plan is to spend a lot of time reading and watching course videos. There are already a few good resources available for eg you can check CMU Database Group on Youtube or read Database Internals by Alex Petrov

I hope you will see something concrete by the end of year. Best of luck to me.
