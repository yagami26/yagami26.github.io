Rook Strategy

Feb 09, 2025, Posted in Chess

I was a little bored of reading the endgames, and wanted to read something different for a change. I was already reading Reassess Your Chess (also by Jeremy Silman). It's a good book, but as per my chess dojo, this book is too advanced and should not be read by my rating range :P. But I think getting exposure to ideas is not a bad thing.

I did not want to read about knights and bishops, so I decided to read about rooks. Also because I do feel I rarely use rooks efficiently in the game. So having a strategy or two for rooks should not be bad. I came across two interesting techniques.

  • Using a piece to create interference to occupy a file.
  • Doubling up rooks just below a pawn which you can exchange, Jeremy mentions it as holding the trigger to a gun.
